Legacy

Jose Rizal’s legacy is deeply embedded in the history and identity of the Philippines. As a nationalist, writer, and reformist, he used his intellect and pen to advocate for peaceful reform during the Spanish colonial era.

His novels Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo exposed the injustices and abuses of the colonial government and the clergy, awakening the Filipino people's sense of nationalism.

Rizal’s execution on December 30, 1896, made him a martyr and intensified the revolutionary movement against Spain.

Beyond his writings, his unwavering commitment to education, freedom, and equality continues to inspire generations.

Today, Jose Rizal is remembered not only as the national hero of the Philippines but also as a symbol of courage, integrity, and intellectual resistance against oppression.
