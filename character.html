Character Analysis

Virtues & Strengths

  • Intellectual brilliance, sharp memory, and love for learning
  • Patriotism, compassion, and sense of justice
  • Discipline, moral courage, artistic and literary talent

Shortcomings & Weaknesses

  • Tendency to prefer gradual reform over armed struggle
  • Sometimes indecisive and overly cautious
  • Physical frailty, especially poor eyesight

Evolution of Character

As a child in Calamba, Rizal displayed curiosity, artistry, and compassion, nurtured by his mother’s teaching and his father’s discipline. His early education at home and in local schools laid the foundation of his brilliance and sensitivity.

During his years in Ateneo, UST, and later in Europe, Rizal matured into a reformist intellectual. Encounters with European ideas, sciences, and arts expanded his worldview. He wrote novels, essays, and poems that gave voice to the oppressed, showing his growth from student to advocate for reform.

Exiled in Dapitan and ultimately condemned in Manila, Rizal’s final years revealed his unwavering sense of duty. He accepted his fate with dignity, evolving from reformer to martyr whose death gave birth to a revolution and cemented his place as the national hero.
